This was a return to my teenage reading years, revisiting the first book
of the *Dragonlance Chronicles*. It had been long enough that I had
forgotten many of the details, which made the story feel fresh again
while still carrying that sense of familiarity. The world-building, the
companions, and the unfolding quest reminded me why these books became
such a staple of fantasy in the first place.

Listening to the audiobook brought a new dimension to the story. Paul
Boehmer’s narration captured the tone well, giving each character a
distinct voice and keeping the pace engaging. It’s not the kind of high
literature that demands deep analysis, but it doesn’t need to be. At its
heart, this is just a fun, classic fantasy tale—and sometimes that’s
exactly what I’m looking for.
