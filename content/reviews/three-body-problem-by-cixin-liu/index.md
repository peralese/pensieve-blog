I picked up The Three-Body Problem. The premise, scientific ideas, and
vast scope are intriguing: from the opening I was fascinated by the
blend of history, physics, and alien contact.

Yet as I moved forward, the book became harder to finish. The pacing
slowed significantly. The middle section felt dense: technical
exposition, shifting timelines, and multiple characters so far removed
that I struggled to keep invested in their arcs. There were stretches
where the scientific framing overshadowed the emotional/motivational
grounding of the characters. It often felt more like reading to
understand ideas than to follow a story I cared about.

So, do I plan to continue the series? I’m still undecided. Part of me
wants to see how the later books deepen these ideas, whether they
balance the science and the human more evenly. But another part
hesitates: will the slog outweigh the lift? Will the next novels move
faster, feel more grounded in character? Right now, I am not sure.
