 The Civil War has always been one of my favorite subjects to read
about, and Bruce Catton remains one of the best at bringing that history
to life. I’ve read several of his works, and this two-volume look at
Ulysses S. Grant did not disappoint.

The first book follows Grant’s rise in the western campaigns, from Fort
Donelson through the long struggle at Vicksburg. Catton captures both
the determination and the setbacks, showing how Grant’s steady
leadership began to shape the course of the war. The second book picks
up after Lincoln appoints him commander of all Union armies, carrying
the story through the brutal campaigns from Chattanooga to the final
surrender at Appomattox.

What I enjoy most in Catton’s writing is how accessible he makes the
history feel. The battles are vividly described, but the focus always
comes back to the people—their choices, their resilience, and the human
cost behind the strategy. I recommend this for anyone who shares an
interest in the Civil War, this set is a rewarding and powerful read.
