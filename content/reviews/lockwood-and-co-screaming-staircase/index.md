I first came across this series through the Netflix adaptation of the
first two books of Jonathan Stroud’s *Lockwood & Co.* — and it was pure
chance that I even turned it on. I was immediately hooked by the first
episode. When Jen came by while I was watching the third or fourth
episode, she asked, "What is that? It looks cool." I told her all about
it, and both she and Sergio were intrigued. So, we went back and started
from episode one together. They both loved it, and like me, were really
disappointed when Netflix canceled it — especially since it was the \#1
show worldwide at the time. Honestly, I was so mad, I canceled my
Netflix subscription and haven’t been back since.

That’s how obsessed I got. After finishing the show, I dove into the
books — all six of them — and now I’m working through the audiobooks as
well. This review is for both the print and audio versions of Book 1,
*The Screaming Staircase.* The audiobook is narrated by British actress
Miranda Raison, who does a fantastic job bringing the story to life. Her
performance captures Lucy Carlyle’s sharpness and heart, while also
giving each character a distinct voice. The book itself is a gripping
and atmospheric supernatural thriller set in an alternate London plagued
by ghosts. Lucy, a young psychic investigator, joins the small but
fearless Lockwood & Co., led by the charming Anthony Lockwood and his
meticulous partner George. They investigate hauntings all across the
city, leading up to the terrifying Combe Carey Hall — home of the
Screaming Staircase. Stroud blends eerie suspense with clever dialogue
and detailed world-building that’s both spooky and surprisingly warm.
It’s a must-read (and listen) for fans of the paranormal, detective
stories, or just good storytelling in general.
