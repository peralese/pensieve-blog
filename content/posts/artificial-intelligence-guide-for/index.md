*Artificial Intelligence: A Guide for Thinking Humans* by Melanie
Mitchell is an accessible and thought-provoking exploration of AI, its
history, capabilities, and limitations. I started listening to this book
on Audible, but the book references a number of images and figures from
the book, and while there is an accompanying pdf, containing those
images, I ended up purchasing the print version as well.   Mitchell is a
computer scientist, and she demystifies AI by explaining key concepts
like machine learning, neural networks, and common misconceptions while
addressing the challenges of achieving true intelligence. She examines
AI's successes and failures, highlighting the gap between human and
machine cognition. Through engaging examples and a balanced perspective,
the book provides a nuanced understanding of AI’s current state and
future potential, making it an essential read for anyone curious about
the field.

While the book is somewhat dated—for instance, it doesn’t cover
Generative AI, which is currently making headlines—it still provides a
compelling overview of key AI concepts, explaining them in a thorough
and accessible manner.   I found the book extremely fascinating and well
worth the time invested.
